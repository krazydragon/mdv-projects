Week 2 Discussion: Accessibility

1. What are some of the ways that you could change your HTML on your Demo app to be more accessible? (Remember, you can use the Section tag with jQuery, for instance)

I would use user friendly font size, style, and color. I would also use the section tag to help separate the different sections in my app so that if someone was using a screen reader it would read the part of the app that mattered the most to the user.

2. Visit a video service on the web (like YouTube) and search for "Screen Reader Demo" and "Mobile Screen Reader" - what are some of the common frustrations (or common support mechanisms) that you see among the examples that are given - how could you fix them (or use them)?

The frustrations that I saw is that sometimes that they don’t work as well as they should due to issues like 3rd party app support and also just having a simple setup. Also some of the voices that they use might be hard for certain people to understand.

3. Devices: Please list two devices (and some of their features) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

Multiple devices come to mind all ios devices and most android devices support Accessibility. They will let you zoom in so that you can read better. You can also get sound and vibration feedback when you touch the screen.

4. Mobile Apps: Please list two apps (either Android or iOS, or both) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

On android you can download talkback, soundback, and kickback. These apps are made by google. Talkback is a screen reader that will tell you whats on the screen. Soundback gives audible feedback, and Kickback gives a vibrating feedback.
